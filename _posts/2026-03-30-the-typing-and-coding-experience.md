manual typing and coding was so fun -- until it becomes inefficient.

<!--more-->

<u>heads up</u>: this post is mostly about vim and the general experience of using it for copy-editing.

for many years when I'm in college, I've never a big fan of **vim**g. I was first introduced to it by one of my teacher -- saying that he can code and edit code so _efficient_ it feels illegal to go back to a non-vim mode.

then he demonstrated that to us.

and well, it was _mediorce_ at best. He was fast and the idea to never leave your keyboard makes sense to me, but the complexity and effort makes it feel kinda not worth it.

I could not be so wrong.

years later, i discovered [ThePrimeagen](https://www.youtube.com/@theprimetimeagen) (go check him out). Apart from his meme software content, I am very amazed by how fast he can code, switch to another file (or buffer, if you will), go to a function signature, go to the terminal, run a command, and so on, while constantly talking and thinking out loud.

**that**, that is what feels right to me.

so I learned vim. I started with [nvim](https://neovim.io/), and then [lazyvim](https://www.lazyvim.org/). From there, it becomes a rabbit hole of personalization and customization: install plugins, enable this, disable that, tinker with the setting, learn Lua (?), etc. I even went further and brought that obsession to my terminal ([Ghostty](https://ghostty.org/), btw), setup a bunch of things to my preferences so I never have to leave it.

and it was **SO FUN**, it really is. I think I've never enjoy working with my machine that much.

and you know what's the best part? The joy extended to my job as well: **coding**.

so you see, vim and vim motion is born for coding (I never fact check this but there are many reasons to believe so). So now, by enjoying working with my machine, I suddenly enjoy working for my job as well (!). And thus, here comes the productivity baby.

or so I thought.

as soon as I'm used to vim-ing everyday comes the age of AI. Manual typing already feels like **a waste of time**. Well I told you, I'm so heavily affected to SNS -- this take prolly comes from X. AI is generating my code, writing my e-mails, answering my messages, scheduling my meetings. I don't even have to type! With many _"semantic"_ on-device speech-to-text (STT) tools, now I can just talk to the AI.

so the original premise is still there: I do things faster, I do not need to constantly switch context, I don't need to leave my keyboard, and I'm being very efficient. But still, I cannot feel same -- quite the opposite even. I feel like I'm being taken away the joy of coding, of typing, of copy-editing.

you know, nothing is more rewarding than spending countless hours to debug your code to finally see it compiles, or spending hours searching your hyperparameters to finally see the loss drops. The dopamine hit is insane.

I know, nothing is preventing me from winding up `nvim` right now and start writing code. But doing something you know is sub-optimal just don't feel that good.

maybe this is just me being conservative and stubborn. But yeah, the experience I've had is amazing, and now it becomes an exotic that bothers me.
