[Hades](https://www.supergiantgames.com/games/hades) was the game that leads me to my current obsession with rouge-lite games, so I had a lot of expectations for [Hades II](https://www.supergiantgames.com/games/hades-ii).

<!--more-->

and I wasn't let down, at least in terms of gameplay and game mechanics. The introduction of **&Omega; moves** adds a whole new layer to the combat depth. The **Casts** and **Specials** now actually can be the main source of damage, instead of just some filler and chip dmg like in the first game. The **weapons/aspects** and the **boons** are playing so well together, allowing a lot of different builds (what the hell was they thinking with _Circe staff_ lol).

overall, it's a great game, and I have definitely spend more time playing it than I have in the first game (comparatively within the similar timeframe).

but well, being a sequel to an excellent game means the bar is now higher.

here comes the rant about its problems.

the first problem I have is rather personal: it's about the **humour and wittiness** of the game. I think this is one of the best thing in **Hades I**. A lot of characters are clever and funny in their own way: Zagreus, Skelly, Homer, Asterius, Theseus, Dionysus, and even Hades himself. That kinda doesn't exist in Hades II? I understand that Mel is not Zag, and the context in the second game is way more serious than the first, but there are obviously many place for some gags. Unfortunately, that's not the case. Homer is boring, the chemistry between Mel and Skelly is not there, the surrounding characters are simply not funny. I can see they try with Odysseus and Eris, but yeah, they are bad and forgettable.

the next problem is the **boss fights**, specifically the **rivals** ones. Imho, half of the boss fights are mediorce at best, or if I'm being honest -- boderline dogsh\*t. C'mon guys, what the hell was that rival **Scylla** fight about? Or in rival **Cerberus**, where you have to _chase the doggo_ for 10,000 miles every 3-4 times in a game where you do not have a spammable dash. And don't let me start talking about the **Eris** fights -- ugh. Why did you give us a god-tier **Origination** just to make **Eris** fly away and reset that every 5 seconds? The other fight I think is bad, but not as much, is rival **Typhon**. I just think the phase where he spawns in the tentacles is a bit too much, and the final phase is just a gimmick. Just as a constrary, I think rival **Hecate** and **Chronos** fights are the best in the games. Very fun and very hard. Nothing gonna top the rival **Furies** fights in Hades I tho, sorry guys.

and now is the biggest, most popular, and most annoying problem: the **ending**. If you don't know, when the game is first released, the ending was different. It made the fanbase so angry that in the end, **Supergiant Games** has to re-written the entire ending. Idk, it's hard to explain so let me just tell you the whole thing (spoiler alert). So basically after Melinoë beats Chronos and Typhon back-to-back many many times, she and Zagreus (in the past) can finally find a solution for all of this: they will track down past Chronos, when he is chained under Tartarus, and kill him right at that moment, before he has _time_ to strengthen up which will lead to current events. A nice plan, and a very nice resolution for what seems to be unsolvable to Mel. But, here comes the plot: Zag decided that instead of kill Chronos for good, he will _convince_ the old grouch to _forget and forgive_ to become a _loving grandfather_. And Chronos is actually convinced (?), thus developing a detached reality where the family of 5, including Mel, lives happily together. And you know what worse? Mel knows _nothing_ about this, and she possess no such happy memory. Her parents didn't believe her of her story. Suddenly, Mel has become an outsider of her own hero arc, witnessing her family living happily with her arch-nemesis, and being denied of her tragic past and her life mission. How cruel this is!

well obviously after this came out in the early access, Supergiant Games has to changed the ending, such that Mel is the one making the decision of helping Chronos and re-live the alternative life. But for me, and prolly for other fans that have experienced the first ending, this still feels like a cheat, a mere patch on a broken glass. Till the moment of writing this, I still cannot believe that was actually what happened.

Hades II is still an excellent game, but I really feel the need to pouring all my problems with it here. I think for a rouge-lite game, only the second problem does matter much, because after all, the core quality of the game is its replayability. Still, for a fan of the franchise, there are many things that would let them down. I hope the studio can learn from this experiences and improve for their next game.
